Cache Concepts from RFC 2616

Cache: A local store of responses used by clients or intermediaries to reduce latency and bandwidth (Sections 1.2, 13).

Freshness: Defines how long a cached response is valid, based on headers like Expires and Cache-Control: max-age (Section 13.2).

Cache-Control: A header that specifies caching directives for requests and responses (Section 14.9).

Validators: Mechanisms such as ETag and Last-Modified that allow revalidation of cached responses (Section 13.3).

Age Header: Indicates how long a response has been stored in caches, in seconds (Section 14.6).
